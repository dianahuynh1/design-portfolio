Visual Transformation

For this project, we chose an architect to create a poster that represented their body of work. I chose Santiago Calatrava for my poster. I drew from his archtitecural works as well as his drawings and sculptures. What I discovered was that he uses a lot of repetition, a limited color palette, and despite being massive buildings and bridges, they all looked as if they were light and airy.

Non-Restrained Typography

"The opposite of restrained typography is not chaotic typography, but instead non-restrained typography: this includes all(!) possible conditions—except of restraint." Based on this quote, I designed a poster that represented non-restrained typography. This poster was also influenced by my interest in astronomy, stars and constellations. I thought that constellations best showed non-restraint — constellations are bunched together based on what they look like and proximity in the sky, while in reality, they are hundreds of thousands of lightyears away from each other and the same bunch of stars could look like anything else besides what they are named after.
